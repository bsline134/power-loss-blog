What is the rock that Jesus builds his church on?

April 30, 2026 • 2 min read

Jesus built his church on Peter's confession of faith. Today, he likewise builds his church on this same faith.

But what exactly is this "faith"? Is it strength, courage? Is it boldness through adversity?

"Faith" is accepting something - it is not a virtue in and of itself. Something is shown to us (or revealed to us, Matthew 16:17), and we accept it.

Think of Abraham. Genesis 15:6 says that Abraham believed the Lord. And? He (the Lord) counted it to him as righteousness.

Belief is a mixture of trust and acceptance. Anyway, my point is that it is not a virtue, or something inherent about us - it is about accepting something in the heart and believing it.

Did Peter trust that Jesus was the Christ, the Son of the living God? Yes, and why is that significant? It is significant because this is the same kind of trust and faith we ourselves need today.

Jesus asked His disciples, "But who do you say that I am?" (Matthew 16:15). May we ourselves confess that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God, and may the Lord be able to find His church on us - on this confession of faith.

May we follow in this leading apostle's footsteps, and bear witnesses to the Lord to the end of the earth.
